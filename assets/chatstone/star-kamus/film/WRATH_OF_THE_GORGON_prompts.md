# STAR KAMUS: WRATH OF THE GORGON
### A Seedance 2 production pack — 1980s B-movie space opera (~3.5 min short film)

Based on the STAR KAMUS game. Every prompt below is self-contained and fits a
15-second generation. Generate the ASSETS first (A1–A18), save each output named
by its ID, then generate the FILM SHOTS (S1–S16) attaching the listed reference
assets for consistency.

---

## THE STYLE BLOCK — paste this VERBATIM at the start of EVERY prompt

> 1980s low-budget sci-fi B-movie, shot on grainy 16mm film stock, slightly
> faded Technicolor with crushed blacks, 4:3 aspect ratio, practical effects
> only: miniature spaceships hanging from faintly visible fishing wire, rubber
> creature suits with visible seams, cardboard control panels with hand-painted
> buttons and blinking Christmas lights, smoke machine haze, hard studio key
> lighting with colored gels (magenta and cyan), occasional boom-mic shadow,
> mono audio hiss, dramatic analog synthesizer score. Earnest, corny,
> completely sincere tone — the actors believe every word.

**Consistency rules:**
1. Use the exact character names and wardrobe phrases below in every prompt — never paraphrase.
2. Attach the relevant reference clips/images (your likeness for Kamus, your commander clip, plus generated assets) to every shot that features them.
3. Keep the same aspect ratio (4:3) everywhere.
4. When a shot mixes characters + ships, prefer cutting into two shots rather than cramming — 15 s is one beat, maybe two.

# PART 2 — THE SHORT FILM, SHOT BY SHOT
*16 shots, each ≤ 15 s. Attach the listed references. Total ≈ 3.5 minutes.*

### S1 — Cold open / title card (15 s) — refs: A17
[STYLE BLOCK] + Slow push through a starfield of hanging foam asteroids.
A booming movie-trailer narrator voice over crackling mono audio: "In the
year 3007... the Gorgon Swarm came for Sector Seven. One pilot stood in
their way." Huge chrome 3D-extruded title text slams onto screen with a
synth blast: "STAR KAMUS: WRATH OF THE GORGON", the letters wobbling

### S2 — Bridge briefing (15 s) — refs: A2 (your commander clip), A13
[STYLE BLOCK] + Aboard the Argo bridge, red alert lights spinning. COMMANDER
ARGO in his navy uniform with amber epaulettes slams his coffee mug down on

Swarm has entered Sector Seven. Scramble our best pilot." A junior officer
off-screen: "Sir... you don't mean—" Commander, jaw clenched, zoom-in:
"Get me KAMUS." Dramatic synth sting, crash zoom to his eyes.

### S3 — Zeraa's intel (15 s) — refs: A4, A13
[STYLE BLOCK] + On the Argo bridge, AMBASSADOR ZERAA in her silver disco

### S5 — Launch! (12 s) — refs: A8, A9, A14, A15

[STYLE BLOCK] + MEDUSA PRIME looms, lava-lamp core seething inside its

### S14 — Vex's last stand (14 s) — refs: A6, A16, A12, A15
[STYLE BLOCK] + Vex rises from his snake throne, cape flaring in the fog,
and slams his staff: "FULL POWER TO THE CORE! CRUSH HIM!" The fortress
core flares white and the whole miniature lunges forward on its rig; in the

### S15 — Core kill & escape (15 s) — refs: A12, A8, A18
[STYLE BLOCK] + The SF-01 dives straight at the exposed lava-lamp core,
launching everything — cyan beams, sparkler torpedoes. The core cracks,
glows, and the entire Medusa Prime miniature detonates in the biggest
slow-motion practical fireball of the film, rings tumbling apart, as the
SF-01 races out of the blast on its wire, ribbon exhaust flat-out. Kamus
screams over the roar: "THAT'S CHECKMATE!" The red nebula clears to
starfield.

### S16 — Homecoming & sequel hook (15 s) — refs: A1, A2, A3, A4, A5, A14, A6
[STYLE BLOCK] + Hangar celebration: confetti cannons, the crew cheering.
The Commander hands Kamus a steaming plate: "As promised. Dinner's on me."
It's a single sad space-hotdog; everyone laughs too hard. Zeraa kisses
Kamus on the cheek leaving glitter; Nova slaps his shoulder; Klorp raises
four flutes of bubbling green liquid. Freeze-frame mid-laugh, synth outro,
credits start rolling — then a final cut to black space, where Vex's clawed
glove drifts among the debris... and slowly clenches into a fist. Title:
"KAMUS WILL RETURN."

## Production tips
- Generate A1–A18 first; keep the best 15 s of each as your reference library.
- For every film shot, attach: your Kamus likeness clip + A1 whenever Kamus
  appears; your commander clip + A2 for the Commander; and the listed ship/set
  assets. More references = better consistency.
- If a shot drifts off-style, prepend the style block twice — repetition helps.
- Keep dialogue exactly as written (it's synced to the game's lines where it
  counts: "systems green", "aim for the head", "that's checkmate", "dinner's
  on me").
- Optional bonus assets if you're having fun: a cantina crowd of rubber-suit
  aliens on the Argo rec deck, a Gorgon drone marching band for the credits,
  a "SECTOR 7 NEWS" anchor desk for a mock newscast cold open.
